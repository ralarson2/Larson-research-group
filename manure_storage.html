Manure Storage

Manure storage systems provide flexibility in nutrient management and play a critical role in reducing risks to water quality and air emissions.

Storage types

Agricultural manure is commonly stored as a solid or as a liquid or slurry. Solid manure is typically stored in stacks or bunkers, while liquid and slurry manure are stored in earthen basins, concrete tanks, or lined structures. Storage design influences emissions, nutrient preservation, and management options.

Why storage matters

Adequate storage capacity allows producers to avoid land application during frozen ground conditions, periods of heavy precipitation, or other times when runoff and leaching risks are elevated. This flexibility is one of the most effective tools for protecting surface and groundwater quality.

Research focus

Our work evaluates how storage type, duration, and management influence nutrient losses, greenhouse gas emissions, and downstream land application outcomes. This includes assessment of covered and uncovered systems, seasonal emptying strategies, and integration with other manure treatment practices.
