Research

Our work spans experimental measurements, modeling, and stakeholder engagement to understand and reduce emissions while maintaining productive, resilient systems.

Manure and nutrient management
We study advanced manure processing and land-application strategies, quantifying greenhouse gas and ammonia emissions, nutrient losses, and recovery opportunities. Our projects evaluate multi-stage separation systems, acidification, and anaerobic digestion, linking on-farm measurements with life cycle assessment.
Short-lived climate pollutants
We evaluate mitigation strategies for methane and other short-lived climate forcers across livestock systems, waste management, and energy. Our work connects feed interventions, storage and application practices, and improved combustion with climate modeling and air quality outcomes.
Decision-support and systems modeling
We build tools and frameworks that make complex tradeoffs transparent for decision makers. This includes optimization of plastic waste management pathways, integration of farm-level tools such as COMET-Farm with detailed LCAs, and scenario analysis for policy and investment decisions.
Collaboration and outreach
We work with farmers, agencies, NGOs, and international partners to co-develop research questions, interpret results in context, and design pragmatic solutions that can actually be implemented.

Teaching and outreach

We connect research with classroom teaching and stakeholder training, emphasizing systems thinking and practical decision support.

Courses include environmental metrics and systems-focused design in agricultural and environmental engineering. Outreach activities involve workshops with farmers and advisors on manure management, short-lived climate pollutants, and integrated waste and energy systems.

We also contribute to legislative briefings, technical trainings, and collaborative projects with local and international partners to translate research into policy-relevant insights.
